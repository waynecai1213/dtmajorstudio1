Final Project Reflection

WEEK 17 - MON, DEC 16TH, 2019

From start to mid-term and final, my intention to research question is actually to critique how modern technology influence human mentally and behaviorally. And after midterm, my specific questions changed to “How to make people aware of the defect of fragmented attention caused by technology?”, with which I started prototyping in 7in7. Even though I have guiding questions for each day’s prototype, those questions are lack of advance planning and structure. So there is somewhat a lack of logic in the prototypes I made in 7in7. My 7in7 is more like brainstorming. With prototypes I made in 7in7, my specific research question became “How would interaction with physical objects bring reflection to/nudge people thus reducing multitasking”.

My project has a relatively high degree of completion in look and feel. The role of this prototype shows fine in the video, but the implementation part is still weak due to my lack of technical knowledge and time. The form that I used to address the problem of multitasking and distraction is novel. The form that products with personality challenge people’s understanding and attention to our relationship with everyday things. My project tries to solve the problems of the moment and takes a future-oriented approach. It would embody relatively advanced technology like computer vision and machine learning. The interaction with such objects is fun and may bring a feeling of being accompanied.

As for the form itself, how to embody their personality through their motion and feedback is critical. The current mechanism is that the objects work as usual when the user focus and they only show personality when the user is distracted. Such feedback may seem to be more of a punishment for the user than encouragement for concentration. This negative reinforcement is sometimes contradictory. The form of my final prototype is actually just from brainstorming, so more rigorous reasoning and testing are still needed, even though I’ve tried to clarify it in the final presentation. After all, many people thought that the lamp itself would be a distraction instead of helping people focus. I may need to figure out my target audiences or take different forms for different user groups. Objects with personalities may also need something else to form an effective mechanism to help users focus, for example, an app or the changes of the light as an interface for a rewarding mechanism. Another issue is that I question myself is that is the problem I tried to solve as broad as multitasking or just addiction to the cellphone. Because currently my prototypes are all based on contexts when the cellphone is a distraction. If it is to solve the problem of distraction or cellphone addiction, how to distinguish it from other products to make it more effective rather than merely novel in form.

The current concept maybe not closely connected to the form. Objects with personalities are an interesting research field to me but applying it to solve the problem of multitasking maybe a little reluctant and narrow. To solve the problem of multitasking and distraction, I may need to think about other more efficient and humanized forms as well as more scenarios. As for objects with personalities, I may reconsider it from a speculative perspective and find its application scenarios and specific user groups. Or to think about what does objects has personality actually mean from a critical point of view.
